Deadly Trio


References




Main Idea

  • Ramsey theorem states that when we use 2-colours to colour the edges in K6, any edge colouring of it would yield a monochromatic triangle.
  • Any complete graph of vertices more than 6 also yields a monochromatic triangle as R(3,3)=6. Therefore, a 2-colouring of K8 and K10 yields a monochromatic triangle.

Goal

  • The name “Deadly-Trio” represents the monochromatic triangle.
  • The plan was to develop a 2-player game that consists of three diffrent levels K6, K8, and K10 which represents Easy, Medium, and Hard modes respectively.
  • Each player will have their own unique colour and on their turn, they have to draw a line between two vertices.
  • According to the rule of the game the player who completes the monochromatic triangle loses.

Rules to Play

  • Initally, The Players needs to choose one from the various different levels.
  • Each player will represent a unique color. Where,

    • First player ------>

      BLUE



    • Second player ------>

      RED



  • On turn each player has to draw a line between two vertices to make a complete graph avoiding a monochromatic triangle.
  • The player who completes the monochromatic triangle loses.

    • NOTE: According to Ramsey Theorem, There will be a player who will be left with no choice other than completing a triangle.
