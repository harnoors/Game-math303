Main Idea


Ramsey theorem states that when we use two-colours to colour the edges in K6, any edge colouring of it would yield a monochromatic triangle.Similarly, in K8 and K9 two-colouring (red and blue) would yield a red K4 or a blue K3.
Idea is to use this fact and implement a game where 2 players would represent a unique colour each and in a round-robin turn pattern, they will colour an edge during their turn. The person who will complete a Monochromatic triangle loses.

Goal

The name “Deadly-Trio” represents the monochromatic triangle, where according to the rule of the game the player who completes the monochromatic triangle loses. Hence, The plan is to develop a 2-player game that consists of three levels, K6, K8 , and K9 which represents easy medium, and hard modes respectively.
Each player will have their own unique colour and on their turn, they have to draw a line between the vertices. According to Ramsey Theorem when we 2-colour K8 , and K9either a monochromatic K3 or K4or similar colour will form. The goal is to avoid a monochromatic triangle or to form a monochromatic square. The player who created a monochromatic triangle loses, or the player who completes monochromatic square first wins.

Rules to Play

The Player needs to choose from the various different levels easy, moderate and hard.
Easy represents K6, Moderate represents K7 and Hard represents K8.
Then the player starts the game.
First player ------> BLUE
Second player ------> RED
On turn each player has to draw a line between the vertices to make a complete graph avoiding a monochromatic triangle.
The player who is left with no choice other than completing a triangle loses.
The Game will consist of several rounds and scores from each round will be added to the scoreboard.
The player with the maximun score wins.
